What Happened

The Senate approved a $1.2 trillion spending package early Tuesday on a 51–49 vote, ending a marathon session that ran past 3 a.m. Two members of the majority crossed the aisle; one absence was recorded.

The bill funds the government through September and includes disaster relief, defense increases, and a contested set of energy provisions. It now heads to the House, where leadership has signaled a vote by Friday.

"This is a compromise nobody loves and everybody can live with." — the Majority Leader, on the floor.
